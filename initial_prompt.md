# Bridging both worlds: Design your historical world

1. Select a historical context.
 - Choose a situation where understanding people’s actions requires attention to their social, economic, or cultural context.
2. Define three agents (characters) with a purpose.
 - Identify three characters who represent different perspectives. For each, specify: who they are; what they want; what constrains them.
3. Articulate a clear learning objective.
 - What should learners understand after engaging with your simulation that they might not have understood before?
4. Describe a key interaction.
 - What interaction between the characters will make the historical context visible and meaningful?
